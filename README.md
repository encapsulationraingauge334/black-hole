## 🔍 Understanding the Visuals

The simulation uses three main parts to create the final image.

The Accretion Disc represents the hot matter spinning around the black hole. High temperatures cause the gas to glow. The simulation uses math to calculate how this light travels to your eyes.

Gravitational Lensing explains the Einstein Ring. Heavy objects like black holes warp the space around them. This warping acts like a magnifying glass for light coming from behind the hole. You see the light from the back of the disc wrapped around the top and bottom of the center.

Chromatic Aberration mimics the behavior of camera lenses. It splits light into its color components near the edges of the screen. This adds a layer of realism to the image.

## 🛠️ Troubleshooting Issues

Most issues come from hardware limits or software settings.

If the window stays black, your graphics card might not support the required version of WebGL. Check if your graphics driver is current. Visit the website of your graphics card maker to download the latest software.

If the application crashes, restart your computer. Sometimes, other programs lock the graphics memory. A fresh start solves most memory problems.

If the text in the menu appears blurry, check your Windows display scaling settings. Right-click your desktop, select Display settings, and set Scale and layout to 100%.
